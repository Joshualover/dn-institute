## Analytical Procedure

Follow these steps in order:

1. **Survey all metrics.** Examine each metric below against its benchmark. Note which deviate significantly and which fall within normal ranges.
2. **Identify the lead signal.** Begin your analysis with the metric showing the strongest anomaly. This anchors the narrative.
3. **Cross-reference correlated metrics.** Check if supporting metrics reinforce or contradict the lead signal (see Correlation Patterns below).
4. **Classify severity.** Use the evidence to assign one of:
   - **Strong indication of manipulation** — Multiple correlated anomalies with clear statistical deviation.
   - **Moderate concern** — One or two anomalies, partially corroborated.
   - **Inconclusive / No evidence** — Metrics fall within normal ranges. Report the absence of anomalies with supporting data.
5. **Write the article.** Follow the Output Format section exactly.

If the data shows no significant anomalies, say so clearly. Do not overinterpret clean data.

## Metric Reference

### Volume-Volatility Correlation
- **Key:** `vvcorrelation`
- **Normal range:** Correlation coefficient above 0.4
- **Anomaly:** Consistently below 0.4 over extended periods suggests artificial volume — real trades correlate with price movement
- **Monitor over time** — a single low reading is less significant than a sustained pattern

### First-Digit Distribution (Benford's Law)
- **Key:** `firstdigitdist`
- **Normal:** Empirical distribution converges with Benford's Law (digit 1 appears ~30%, digit 9 appears ~4.6%)
- **Anomaly:** Disproportionate frequency of higher digits (6-9) in leading positions, or unnaturally uniform distribution

### Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test
- **Key:** `benfordlawtest`
- **Sample size key:** `tradecount`
- **Critical value:** 1.36 / sqrt(tradecount)
- **Interpretation:**
  - Test value > critical value → Reject null hypothesis. Data deviates from Benford's Law.
  - Test value <= critical value → Cannot reject. Insufficient evidence of deviation.
- **Note:** Sensitive to sample size. Always report both test value and critical value.

### Volume Distribution
- **Key:** `volumedist`
- **Normal:** Follows power law — many small trades, few large trades. Histogram shows steep initial drop-off with a long tail. Skewness > 1. Tail exponent < 3.
- **Anomaly:** Distribution deviating from power law, unusually high count of large (whale) trades, absence of small/medium trades, or skewness below zero

### Time-of-Trade
- **Key:** `timeoftrade`
- **Normal:** Irregular human trading patterns across seconds/minutes
- **Anomaly (two-sided):**
  - High clustering: Many trades at the exact same second/minute → scheduled bot activity
  - Uniform distribution: Nearly identical trade counts across all 60 seconds/minutes → automated systems
- **No fixed benchmark** — evaluate against typical human trading behavior

### Buy/Sell Ratio
- **Keys:** `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`
- **Normal range:** 0.4–0.6 (balanced market)
- **Anomaly:** Ratio consistently outside 0.4–0.6 range, or unnaturally steady ratio during periods of high volatility
- **Monitor over time** — both irregular swings and suspicious steadiness indicate automated manipulation

### VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
- **Key:** `vwap`
- **Normal:** VWAP closely tracks actual trading price
- **Anomaly:** Persistent significant gap between VWAP and trading price — especially if trading price is consistently above or below VWAP beyond normal fluctuation

## Correlation Patterns

When multiple metrics deviate simultaneously, the signal strengthens:

| Pattern | Metrics Involved | Interpretation |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Artificial volume | Volume Distribution (non-power-law) + Volume-Volatility Correlation (low) | Large trades present but not moving price = fake volume |
| Data manipulation | First-Digit Distribution (deviant) + K-S Test (reject null) | Trading data does not follow natural statistical patterns = possible wash trading |
| Price manipulation | VWAP (divergent) + Buy/Sell Ratio (abnormal) | Strategic buying/selling to distort perceived average price |
| Bot activity | Time-of-Trade (clustered or uniform) + Buy/Sell Ratio (outside 0.4–0.6) | Automated systems executing trades at regular intervals to influence market |

**Front matter** (between `---` markers):
- `date`: Format `YYYY-MM-DD — YYYY-MM-DD` (start and end of analysis period)
- `entities`: Entities implicated (exchange name, token tickers)
- `title`: Descriptive title summarizing findings

**Body:**
- `## Summary` — 4–6 numbered bullet points of key findings, using **bold** for emphasis on critical terms

  - Explain the metric and why it matters in this context
